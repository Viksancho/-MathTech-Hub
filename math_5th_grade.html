1. **Number Sense and Operations:** - Understand and apply place value up to millions. - Perform addition, subtraction, multiplication, and division of whole numbers fluently. - Understand and apply properties of operations (commutative, associative, distributive). 2. **Fractions and Decimals:** - Compare and order fractions and decimals. - Add, subtract, multiply, and divide fractions and decimals. - Understand the relationship between fractions and decimals. 3. **Algebraic Concepts:** - Solve simple equations involving one variable (e.g., 2x + 5 = 11). - Identify patterns and relationships, including arithmetic and geometric sequences. - Use variables to represent unknown quantities in word problems. 4. **Geometry:** - Classify and identify properties of two-dimensional shapes (e.g., polygons, triangles, quadrilaterals). - Understand and apply concepts of perimeter, area, and volume. - Identify lines of symmetry and perform basic transformations (translations, reflections). 5. **Measurement:** - Convert between different units of measurement (e.g., length, weight, capacity). - Solve problems involving time, money, and measurement. - Understand and apply concepts of perimeter, area, and volume to real-world situations. 6. **Data and Statistics:** - Collect, organize, and represent data using tables, graphs (bar graphs, line plots), and charts. - Interpret data and draw conclusions. - Understand basic concepts of probability and make predictions based on data. 7. **Mathematical Reasoning:** - Solve multi-step word problems involving all four operations. - Apply critical thinking and problem-solving strategies to mathematical tasks. - Explain and justify mathematical thinking and solutions. These foundational concepts in mathematics provide students with the necessary skills and knowledge to tackle more advanced topics in algebra, geometry, and statistics in subsequent grades. Mastery of these concepts by the end of 5th grade sets a strong foundation for continued mathematical learning and application in higher grades and beyond.

Numbers

1. **Number Sense and Operations:** - Read and write numbers up to 100,000. - Locate, compare, and order numbers using both regular (standard) and expanded forms. - Understand place value up to 100,000, including decimals to the thousandths place. - Count by multiples (3's, 4's, 6's, 7's, 8's, 9's, 10's, 11's, and 12's) up to 144. 2. **Multiplication and Division:** - Memorize multiplication facts up to the 12 times tables. - Understand division facts related to multiplication. 3. **Decimals:** - Understand decimals to the thousandths (e.g., 0.013). - Add, subtract, multiply, and divide decimals. 4. **Fractions:** - Demonstrate a solid understanding of fractions and their decimal equivalents up to hundredths (e.g., 1/4 = 0.25). - Perform multiplication and division of fractions. 5. **Problem-Solving and Mathematical Thinking:** - Communicate mathematical thinking clearly in problem-solving situations. - Select appropriate problem-solving strategies for addition, subtraction, multiplication, and division in word problems. These skills collectively form a comprehensive foundation in mathematics, emphasizing numerical operations, understanding of decimals and fractions, and proficient problem-solving abilities. Mastering these concepts equips students with essential mathematical skills necessary for further academic success.

Measurements

1. **Units of Measurement:** - Understand and apply inches, feet, yards, miles, millimeters, centimeters, meters, and kilometers in problem-solving activities. - Determine appropriate units of measure and make accurate estimations. - Construct or illustrate items using a variety of units of measure. 2. **Estimation and Accuracy:** - Estimate and round measurements accurately. 3. **Dates and Money:** - Read and write dates using different formats (e.g., Jan. 10, 2002, 02/10/02). - Understand money amounts up to $1000.00 for making change and solving problems. 4. **Measurement Problems:** - Investigate and solve problems involving: - Circumference and perimeter of geometric shapes (e.g., circles, polygons). - Volume and capacity of solids and containers. - Area of two-dimensional shapes using appropriate formulas. 5. **Application of Formulas:** - Explain and apply rules and formulas for calculating circumference, perimeter, volume, capacity, and area. These skills and concepts provide a solid foundation in measurement and applied mathematics, enabling students to solve real-world problems involving various units of measure, dates, money, and geometric calculations.

Geometry

1. **Geometric Shapes and Figures:** - Identify, sort, and classify geometric shapes (e.g., polygons, circles, prisms). - Construct and measure geometric figures accurately using appropriate tools. 2. **Geometric Properties and Relationships:** - Demonstrate a full understanding of geometric properties such as sides, angles, vertices, and faces of shapes. - Identify and describe relationships between geometric figures (e.g., congruence, similarity). 3. **Triangles:** - Classify triangles based on side properties (e.g., scalene, isosceles, equilateral) and angle properties (e.g., acute, obtuse, right). - Measure and construct triangles using a protractor and ruler. 4. **3-Dimensional Figures:** - Identify and construct 3-dimensional solids (e.g., cubes, prisms, pyramids). - Recognize and construct 2-D nets that represent 3-D solids. 5. **Angles and Protractor:** - Measure and construct a variety of angles using a protractor. - Understand the relationship between degrees and angles (acute, obtuse, right angles). 6. **Tiling Patterns and Tessellations:** - Explore and identify tiling patterns that cover a plane (tessellations) using geometric shapes. - Construct and analyze different types of tessellations. 7. **Coordinate System:** - Understand and apply the coordinate system on maps and grids. - Plot points and locate objects using coordinates (x, y). These skills and concepts provide a comprehensive understanding of geometry at the 5th-grade level, preparing students to solve geometric problems and explore spatial relationships both in practical and abstract contexts.

Algebra/Patterning

1. **Patterns:** - Identify, create, analyze, and extend patterns using numbers, shapes, or objects. - Describe the rules or sequences that govern the patterns. - Explore patterns involving more than one variable (e.g., patterns with increasing or decreasing sequences). 2. **Equations and Operations:** - Determine the values of missing terms in equations involving addition, subtraction, multiplication, and division. - Provide rules or strategies to solve equations with missing terms or operations. - Solve equations that involve more than one operation (e.g., \(3 + 2 \times x = 10\)) by determining the value of the variable. 3. **Equivalence in Equations:** - Demonstrate understanding of equivalence in equations involving the four operations. - Identify equivalent expressions or equations (e.g., \(4 + 3 = 7\) is equivalent to \(7 = 4 + 3\)). - Solve for variables in equations to demonstrate equivalence (e.g., \(5x = 20\) leads to \(x = 4\)). These concepts help students develop foundational algebraic skills, including problem-solving strategies, understanding of equations, and proficiency in identifying and extending patterns. They are essential for building a strong mathematical foundation and preparing students for more advanced algebraic concepts in later grades.

Probability

1. **Designing Surveys and Collecting Data:** - Designing effective surveys to collect data on specific topics or questions. - Properly collecting and recording data from surveys or experiments. - Discussing the findings from the collected data and drawing conclusions. 2. **Constructing and Interpreting Graphs:** - Constructing various types of graphs such as bar graphs, line graphs, and pie charts to represent data. - Labeling graphs appropriately with titles, axis labels, and units. - Understanding the differences between different types of graphs and selecting the most appropriate graph for different types of data. 3. **Real-World Applications of Data:** - Discussing the importance and real-world applications of collecting and analyzing data. - Exploring how data collection is used in various fields such as science, economics, and social studies. 4. **Reading, Analyzing, and Interpreting Data:** - Reading and interpreting data presented in graphs, tables, and charts. - Analyzing trends and patterns in data. - Drawing conclusions and making predictions based on data analysis. 5. **Using Tree Diagrams and Probability:** - Using tree diagrams to organize and represent data or outcomes of experiments. - Making decisions based on data organized in tree diagrams. - Conducting probability experiments and applying logical reasoning to predict outcomes. - Predicting probabilities based on prior knowledge or background information. These skills help students develop a solid understanding of data analysis, graphical representation, and probability concepts, which are foundational for further mathematical and scientific learning.
