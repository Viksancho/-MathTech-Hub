Numbers

Understanding Numbers: Read, write, compare, and order whole numbers up to 1,000,000,000. Understand and use place value to read, write, and describe numbers to billions and thousandths. Work with integers, including understanding positive and negative numbers. Operations: Master the four basic operations (addition, subtraction, multiplication, and division) with whole numbers, fractions, and decimals. Understand and compute ratios, proportions, and percentages. Solve problems involving the addition, subtraction, multiplication, and division of fractions and mixed numbers. Number Theory: Identify and use prime numbers, composite numbers, and factors. Calculate greatest common factors (GCF) and least common multiples (LCM).

Measurement

Units and Conversions: Understand and use metric and customary units of measure. Convert between different units of measure within the same measurement system (e.g., centimeters to meters, inches to feet). Perimeter, Area, and Volume: Calculate the perimeter and area of various geometric figures including triangles, rectangles, and composite shapes. Calculate the volume and surface area of rectangular prisms. Precision and Estimation: Measure with accuracy and precision. Make reasonable estimates for measurements and understand when to use estimation versus exact calculations.

Geometry

Properties of Shapes: Identify, classify, and understand the properties of two-dimensional shapes (e.g., triangles, quadrilaterals, polygons) and three-dimensional shapes (e.g., prisms, cylinders, cones). Recognize and describe relationships among geometric shapes, such as congruence and similarity. Coordinate Geometry: Plot points, lines, and shapes on a coordinate plane. Understand and use ordered pairs to locate points in all four quadrants of the coordinate plane. Transformations: Perform and describe transformations of shapes, including translations, rotations, reflections, and dilations.

Algebra/Patterning

Expressions and Equations: Write and evaluate algebraic expressions. Understand and use variables to represent unknowns in expressions and equations. Solve one-step and two-step linear equations and inequalities. Patterns and Functions: Identify and analyze patterns and relationships. Use tables, graphs, and rules to describe linear relationships. Understand the concept of a function and use function notation.

Probability and Statistics

Data Collection and Representation: Collect, organize, and display data in various forms including tables, line plots, bar graphs, and histograms. Understand and interpret data from statistical graphs and charts. Statistical Measures: Calculate and interpret measures of central tendency (mean, median, mode) and variability (range, interquartile range, mean absolute deviation). Probability: Understand and apply basic concepts of probability. Calculate probabilities of simple and compound events. Use probability to make predictions and informed decisions.
