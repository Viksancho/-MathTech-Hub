Numbers

Reading and Comparing Numbers: Read, locate, compare, order, represent, estimate, and identify numbers up to 1,000,000. Understand and use place value to the right and left of zero up to four places. Count by 1s, 2s, 3s, 5s, 10s, and beyond. Arithmetic Operations: Master multiplication facts up to 12 × 12 and related division facts. Understand and work with decimals to the thousandths place. Add, subtract, multiply, and divide whole numbers, fractions, and decimals. Understand and explain the procedures for these operations in problem-solving contexts. Fractions and Decimals: Convert between fractions, decimals, and percents. Demonstrate a solid understanding of fractions and their decimal equivalents.

Measurement

Measurement Concepts: Fully understand and use units of measurement including inches, feet, yards, miles, millimeters, centimeters, meters, and kilometers. Measure accurately and make appropriate estimations for different units of measure. Construct or illustrate items using various units of measure. Estimate and round accurately. Time and Money: Read and write dates in various formats (e.g., January 10, 2002; 01/10/02). Handle money amounts up to $1,000.00, make change, and solve related problems. Understand and solve measurement problems involving circumference, perimeter, volume, capacity, and area, and explain the rules and formulas used.

Geometry

Geometric Shapes and Properties: Identify, sort, classify, construct, measure, and apply a variety of geometric shapes and figures. Fully understand geometric properties and relationships. Classify triangles by side properties and types (e.g., obtuse, isosceles). Geometric Constructions: Identify 2-D nets for 3-D solids and construct the nets. Measure and construct various triangles and angles with a protractor. Explore and discover tiling patterns and tessellations. Understand the coordinate system on maps and grids.

Algebra/Patterning

Pattern Recognition: Identify, create, analyze, and extend patterns and describe the rules with more than one variable. Write algebraic equations/expressions and understand simple formulas. Evaluate simple linear algebraic expressions using one variable. Solve and simplify algebraic equations with the four operations. Substitute natural numbers for variables in algebraic equations. Demonstrate equivalence in equations involving the four operations.
