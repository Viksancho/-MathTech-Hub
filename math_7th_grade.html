1. **Numbers:** - Understanding and working with rational numbers (positive and negative fractions, decimals, and integers). - Operations with rational numbers (addition, subtraction, multiplication, division). - Understanding and applying properties of operations. 2. **Measurements:** - Conversion between different units of measurement (length, weight, capacity). - Solving problems involving measurement conversions. - Understanding formulas for calculating area, perimeter, and volume. 3. **Geometry:** - Properties and classification of geometric figures (angles, triangles, quadrilaterals). - Understanding and applying geometric transformations (translations, rotations, reflections). - Calculating the area and circumference of circles. - Understanding basic concepts of 3-dimensional shapes and their properties. 4. **Algebra:** - Understanding and solving equations and inequalities involving one variable. - Graphing linear equations on a coordinate plane. - Understanding and applying properties of operations to generate equivalent expressions. 5. **Probability:** - Understanding basic probability concepts (likelihood of events, experimental and theoretical probability). - Calculating probabilities of simple events and compound events. - Understanding and applying the concept of independence and dependence of events. Mastery of these concepts ensures that students have a solid foundation in mathematics as they progress to higher grade levels. These topics are fundamental for developing problem-solving skills, logical thinking, and mathematical reasoning essential for further mathematical study.

Numbers

1. **Factors, Multiples, Integer Amounts, and Square Roots:** - Identify factors (numbers that divide evenly into a given number), multiples (numbers that a given number can be divided by), integer amounts (whole numbers and their opposites), and square roots (the number that can be multiplied by itself to give a product). 2. **Comparison and Ordering:** - Compare and order decimals, fractions, and integers using appropriate symbols (<, >, =). 3. **Operations with Integers:** - Add and subtract integers, including understanding the rules for adding and subtracting positive and negative numbers. 4. **Multi-Step Word Problems:** - Solve multi-step word problems that involve factors, multiples, integers, and square roots. Apply these operations to real-world situations. 5. **Fractions, Decimals, and Percents:** - Add, subtract, multiply, and divide fractions. Convert between fractions, decimals, and percents. 6. **Explanation and Justification:** - Explain and justify the procedures used for the above operations in problem-solving. Understand why certain methods are chosen and how they relate to the concepts being applied. These skills enable students to develop a strong understanding of numbers and their relationships, as well as proficiency in performing operations and solving problems involving fractions, decimals, integers, and other related concepts. They are essential for building a solid mathematical foundation and preparing for more advanced mathematics in higher grades.

Measurements

1. **Measurement Terms and Applications:** - Use measurement terms appropriately in both practical and mathematical contexts. - Measure a variety of items at home and at school using standard units of measurement (e.g., meters, centimeters, grams, kilograms). 2. **Solving Complex Measurement Problems:** - Solve more complex problems involving measurement estimations and calculations using a variety of formulas. - Apply measurement skills to real-world scenarios, such as calculating dimensions for construction projects or determining material quantities. 3. **Area Calculations:** - Estimate and calculate areas for various geometric shapes using the correct formulas: - Trapezoids: \( A = \frac{1}{2} \times (b_1 + b_2) \times h \) - Parallelograms: \( A = base \times height \) - Triangles: \( A = \frac{1}{2} \times base \times height \) - Prisms (including rectangles as bases): \( A = \text{perimeter of base} \times \text{height} \) - Circles: \( A = \pi \times r^2 \) (where \( r \) is the radius) 4. **Volume Calculations and Sketching Prisms:** - Estimate and calculate volumes for prisms (rectangular solids) using the formula \( V = \text{base area} \times \text{height} \). - Sketch prisms (rectangular solids) given their volumes, understanding the relationship between volume and dimensions. These skills empower students to apply mathematical concepts to real-world situations involving measurements and geometric shapes. They develop proficiency in calculating areas and volumes, understanding formulas, and making practical estimations, which are fundamental for further studies in mathematics and related fields.

Geometry

1. **Geometric Shapes and Figures:** - Hypothesize about geometric shapes and figures based on given conditions or properties. - Sketch, identify, sort, classify, construct, and measure various geometric shapes and figures, such as triangles, quadrilaterals, polygons, circles, and 3-dimensional figures like cubes and prisms. - Apply geometric principles to solve problems involving these shapes and figures. 2. **Sketching and Constructing Shapes:** - Sketch and construct a variety of shapes given specific dimensions or properties, using tools such as rulers, compasses, and protractors to ensure accuracy. 3. **Problem-Solving with Geometry:** - Create and solve a variety of geometric problems that involve properties of shapes, calculations of areas and volumes, and applications of geometric formulas. 4. **Transformations and Congruence:** - Analyze and identify shapes that have undergone transformations: - Rotations: Turning a shape around a fixed point. - Reflections: Flipping a shape over a line. - Translations: Sliding a shape without rotating or resizing it. - Describe shapes that are congruent (identical in shape and size) after transformations. 5. **Tiling and Tessellations:** - Determine if shapes or figures can tile a plane (tessellate), covering a surface with no gaps or overlaps. - Analyze different types of tiling patterns, including regular tessellations (using identical shapes with no gaps or overlaps) and irregular tessellations (using different shapes with no gaps or overlaps). These skills enable students to deepen their understanding of geometric concepts, develop problem-solving abilities, and apply geometric principles to practical scenarios. Mastery of these concepts prepares students for more advanced geometry and mathematics courses in higher grades.

Algebra/Patterning

1. **Extending and Analyzing Patterns:** - Extend patterns to more complex levels, identifying and justifying the rules that govern them. - Analyze patterns to understand how they evolve and predict future terms or values. 2. **Writing Algebraic Equations/Expressions:** - Write algebraic equations or expressions to represent relationships between variables or quantities in problem-solving contexts. - Write statements to interpret and understand simple formulas or expressions. 3. **Evaluating Linear Algebraic Expressions:** - Evaluate a variety of simple linear algebraic expressions involving one variable and first-degree operations (addition, subtraction, multiplication, division). 4. **Solving and Simplifying Algebraic Equations:** - Solve algebraic equations that involve the four operations (addition, subtraction, multiplication, division) to isolate the variable. - Simplify algebraic expressions by combining like terms and applying distributive property where applicable. 5. **Substituting Natural Numbers for Variables:** - Substitute natural numbers (whole numbers) for variables in algebraic equations or expressions to solve for unknowns. These skills build a foundational understanding of algebraic thinking and problem-solving, preparing students for more complex algebraic concepts in subsequent grades. Mastery of these concepts enables students to model real-world situations, analyze patterns, and develop critical thinking skills essential for higher-level mathematics.

Probability

1. **Designing Surveys and Collecting Data:** - Design surveys to gather information on specific topics or questions of interest. - Collect and organize more complex data from surveys, experiments, or real-world scenarios. 2. **Identifying and Explaining Patterns and Trends:** - Identify patterns and trends in collected data, such as relationships between variables or changes over time. - Explain observed patterns and trends using appropriate statistical language and concepts. 3. **Constructing and Selecting Graphs:** - Construct a variety of graphs, including bar graphs, line graphs, pie charts, and histograms, to represent different types of data. - Label graphs appropriately with titles, axis labels, and units, and explain the reasons for selecting one type of graph over another based on the nature of the data. 4. **Defending Graph Choices and Making Predictions:** - Defend the choices of graphs based on the data being represented and the insights needed from the graph. - Make more accurate predictions based on data trends and patterns observed in the graphs. 5. **Understanding Statistics in Decision Making:** - Understand the importance of statistics in decision-making processes across various fields, such as business, medicine, sports, and public policy. - Provide real-life scenarios where statistical analysis influences decision making. 6. **Describing and Analyzing Data:** - Describe collected data in terms of central tendencies (mean, median, mode) and variability (range, interquartile range). - Analyze any biases in data collection methods and how biases may impact the interpretation of results. 7. **Making Inferences, Predictions, and Evaluations:** - Make inferences and predictions based on interpretations of data collection results, considering uncertainties and variability. - Evaluate the reliability and validity of predictions and conclusions drawn from statistical analyses. 8. **Applying Probability Concepts:** - Predict possible outcomes of events based on background information and apply the rules of probability to games of chance and sports scenarios. - Understand basic concepts of probability, such as calculating probabilities of simple events and using probability to make decisions. These skills equip students with essential statistical and probabilistic reasoning abilities, enabling them to analyze data critically, make informed decisions, and apply mathematical concepts to real-world situations effectively.
